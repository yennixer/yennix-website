Custom & ODM Seals Solutions

YENNIX specializes in custom mechanical seal solutions for applications where standard catalog seals simply won't work. Whether you have an obsolete pump model, a unique equipment configuration, or require an OEM-branded seal for your product line, our engineering team can design and manufacture the exact seal you need.

Our custom seal program combines reverse engineering expertise with modern manufacturing capabilities. Using CMM measurement, 3D CAD modeling, and rapid prototyping, we can transform a worn sample or broken seal into a production-ready replacement — typically within 15-25 days from sample receipt. We handle everything from material selection to performance testing.

We also offer full ODM and private label services for pump manufacturers, seal distributors, and maintenance companies. Your brand, your packaging, your specifications — manufactured to YENNIX quality standards with competitive pricing and reliable lead times. Minimum order quantities are flexible, starting from just 10 sets for custom designs.

FAQ

Common Questions

Component seals consist of individual seal parts (faces, springs, elastomers) that are assembled directly onto the pump shaft during installation. They're generally more economical but require careful installation. Cartridge seals come fully pre-assembled on a sleeve with a gland plate, making installation faster and virtually error-proof. Cartridge seals typically have higher initial cost but lower total cost of ownership due to reduced installation failures and longer service life.

Yes — most YENNIX seal models are designed as direct form, fit, and function replacements for the equivalent models from major brands like Burgmann, John Crane, AESSEAL, Flowserve, and Vulcan. Dimensions, materials, and performance characteristics are matched to ensure interchangeability. Use our Cross Reference tool to find the YENNIX equivalent for your current seal.

Material selection depends on your process fluid, temperature, pressure, and any regulatory requirements. Key factors: seal face material (SiC for general use, TC for abrasive service, Carbon for dry-run capability), elastomer material (Viton for hydrocarbons, EPDM for water/steam, FFKM for aggressive chemicals), and metal components (304SS standard, 316SS for corrosion, Hastelloy for extreme chemical service). Contact our engineers for material recommendations based on your specific application.
