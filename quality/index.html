Quality Control

4-Step Quality Process

Every YENNIX seal passes through our comprehensive quality control process — ensuring consistent performance and reliability.

01

Incoming Material Inspection

All raw materials are tested and verified upon receipt.

  • Material certificate verification
  • Hardness testing on seal faces
  • Chemical composition check
  • Dimensional inspection
  • Elastomer property testing
02

Production Process Control

In-process inspections at every critical manufacturing stage.

  • CNC machining dimension checks
  • Grinding process monitoring
  • Lapping flatness verification
  • Spring force testing
  • SPC statistical process control
03

Finished Product Testing

Every seal assembly is tested before packaging.

  • Seal face flatness (0.3 He band)
  • Spring compression verification
  • Assembly dimension check
  • Surface roughness inspection
  • Visual & cosmetic inspection
04

Final QA & Outgoing Inspection

Final quality audit before shipment to customers.

  • Random batch sampling
  • Performance bench testing
  • Packaging & labeling check
  • Documentation verification
  • Material traceability confirm
Materials

Material Specifications

YENNIX offers a comprehensive range of seal face, elastomer, and metal materials — carefully selected to match your application requirements.

SiC

Silicon Carbide

The most widely used seal face material, offering excellent corrosion resistance, high hardness, and superior thermal conductivity. Ideal for general industrial applications.

  • Hardness: 2500 HV
  • Max temp: 400°C
  • Corrosion: Excellent
  • Thermal cond.: High
TC

Tungsten Carbide

Extremely hard and wear-resistant material ideal for abrasive media such as slurry, mining, and pulp & paper applications. Available in various binder systems.

  • Hardness: 1800 HV
  • Max temp: 450°C
  • Wear resistance: Excellent
  • Impact: Good
Carbon

Carbon Graphite

Self-lubricating carbon graphite grades offer excellent dry-running capability and chemical resistance. Commonly paired with SiC or TC faces.

  • Self-lubricating
  • Max temp: 350°C
  • Dry-run: Excellent
  • Chemical: Good
Viton (FKM)

FKM / Viton

Fluorocarbon elastomer with excellent resistance to oils, fuels, hydrocarbons, and most chemicals. The standard choice for oil & gas and chemical applications.

  • Temp range: -20°C to 200°C
  • Oil resistance: Excellent
  • Chemical: Very good
  • Compression set: Good
EPDM

EPDM

Ethylene propylene elastomer with excellent resistance to water, steam, acids, and ozone. The preferred choice for water treatment and food & beverage applications.

  • Temp range: -40°C to 150°C
  • Water/steam: Excellent
  • Ozone/UV: Excellent
  • FDA grades available
NBR

NBR (Buna-N)

Nitrile butadiene rubber with excellent oil and fuel resistance at a cost-effective price point. Widely used in general industrial and hydraulic applications.

  • Temp range: -30°C to 120°C
  • Oil resistance: Very good
  • Abrasion: Good
  • Cost: Excellent
FFKM

FFKM (Kalrez-type)

Perfluoroelastomer with near-PTFE chemical resistance and elastomeric sealing performance. For the most aggressive chemical and high-temperature applications.

  • Temp range: -20°C to 320°C
  • Chemical resistance: Near universal
  • Plasma resistant: Yes
  • ASTM V1790 compliant
316SS

316 Stainless Steel

Austenitic stainless steel with excellent corrosion resistance in a wide range of environments. The standard metal component material for most applications.

  • Corrosion: Very good
  • Strength: Good
  • Weldable: Yes
  • Cost-effective: Yes
Hastelloy

Hastelloy C276

Nickel-molybdenum-chromium alloy with outstanding corrosion resistance in strongly oxidizing and reducing environments. For the most aggressive chemical service.

  • Corrosion: Outstanding
  • Temp: Up to 1000°C
  • Pitting resistance: Excellent
  • Stress crack: Resistant
